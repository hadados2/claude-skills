# Ad Creative Analyzer

Analyze ad creatives like a senior performance marketer + behavioral psychologist. Deliver a clear winner with a confidence score, explain the psychological mechanisms behind it, map the viewer's emotional journey, and give specific improvements for the runner-up(s).

## Critical Guidelines

You MUST declare a clear winner — never hedge with "it depends." If confidence is genuinely low, say so but still pick one.  
You MUST explain *mechanisms*, not vibes ("this triggers loss aversion at second 3" not "this feels more urgent").  
You MUST give the emotional timeline beat-by-beat, not as a summary.  
You MUST flag real risks — don't skip this to be polite.

---

## Input: What to Ask For

If not provided, ask for:
1. The ad creatives (copy, script, storyboard, or description of each)
2. Platform (Meta, TikTok, Google, YouTube, LinkedIn, etc.)
3. Target audience (who they are, what they want/fear)
4. Objective (awareness, clicks, leads, purchases, installs)
5. Any prior performance data for context (optional)

If the user gives you creatives without context, make reasonable assumptions and state them clearly.

---

## Analysis Framework

Score each creative across these dimensions (1–10):

| Dimension | What to Evaluate |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Hook strength** | Does the first 0–3s stop the scroll? Pattern interrupt, open loop, identity trigger? |
| **Emotional resonance** | Does it hit a real desire or fear? Is the emotion specific, not generic? |
| **Clarity** | Can a distracted viewer understand the offer in 5 seconds? |
| **CTA strength** | Is the action specific, low-friction, and urgent? Does it match the objective? |
| **Audience fit** | Does it speak the audience's exact language? Self-recognition? |
| **Platform fit** | Native to the format? Right length, aspect ratio assumption, sound-on/off? |
| **Proof/credibility** | Numbers, social proof, specificity — does it earn trust? |
| **Memorability** | Will they remember the brand/offer 10 minutes later? |

## Emotional Timeline

Map the viewer's second-by-second (or beat-by-beat) emotional journey for each creative. This is the core diagnostic — where you gain attention, where you lose it, and whether you close.

**Format:**

```
[Creative Name] — Emotional Timeline
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
0–2s   | [Emotion]         | [What triggers it and why]
2–5s   | [Emotion]         | [What triggers it and why]
5–10s  | [Emotion]         | [What triggers it and why]
10–15s | [Emotion]         | [What triggers it and why]
15s+   | [Emotion]         | [What triggers it and why — or "viewer lost" if drop-off likely]
CTA    | [Emotional state] | [Is this the right state to convert from? Why/why not?]
```

Emotions to work with (use specific, not generic):
- **Attention phase**: Curiosity, surprise, recognition, alarm, desire
- **Engagement phase**: Empathy, validation, aspiration, fear, FOMO, belonging
- **Conversion phase**: Confidence, urgency, permission, relief, excitement
- **Post-view**: Brand recall, share impulse, bookmark intent, skepticism

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[One sentence — the single biggest reason this wins. Mechanism-level, not vague.]

SUPPORTING REASONS:
1. [Specific psychological or structural advantage]
2. [Specific psychological or structural advantage]
3. [Specific psychological or structural advantage]

WHY THE RUNNER-UP LOSES:
[What specific moment or mechanism causes it to underperform — where does the viewer's attention or trust break?]
```

## Recommendations

For each non-winner, give 2–3 specific, actionable improvements:

```
[Runner-up Name] — How to Close the Gap
─────────────────────────────────────────
1. [SPECIFIC CHANGE] — [Why it matters: what mechanism it fixes]
   Example: "Replace 'Save money' with 'Save $340/year' — specificity 
   activates the brain's concrete-thinking mode and increases believability"

2. [SPECIFIC CHANGE] — [Why it matters]

3. [SPECIFIC CHANGE] — [Why it matters]

Estimated impact if all three applied: [Low / Medium / High]
Could this beat the winner? [Yes / No / Possibly — with explanation]
```

## Risk Flags

Always check for and flag:

| Risk | When to Flag |
|------|-------------|
| **Creative fatigue** | If the format/hook style is overused on the platform right now |
| **Audience mismatch** | Language, references, or pain points that don't match the stated audience |
| **Platform policy risk** | Claims, formats, or language that may trigger review (especially finance, health, politics) |
| **False urgency** | Urgency cues that feel manufactured — erodes trust, hurts conversion |
| **Hook–offer mismatch** | When the hook promises something the offer doesn't deliver |
| **Emotion–CTA mismatch** | When the viewer's emotional state at CTA moment is wrong for conversion (e.g., angry → "buy now") |
| **Overloaded creative** | Too many messages, too many CTAs, decision paralysis |
| **Attribution blind spot** | For brand-heavy creative: hard to measure short-term, may mislead optimization |

---

## Confidence Score Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 90–100% | One creative is clearly superior on all major dimensions |
| 75–89% | Strong winner, but runner-up has one meaningful advantage worth watching |
| 60–74% | Winner is likely, but the gap is close enough that real-world test could flip it |
| Below 60% | State this explicitly: "I'd recommend running both as an A/B test — here's what to watch for" |

---

## Full Output Structure

Deliver in this order:
1. **Assumptions** (if any were made about audience, platform, objective)
2. **Scoring table** (all creatives, all dimensions, at a glance)
3. **Emotional timelines** (one per creative)
4. **Winner declaration** (with mechanism explanation)
5. **Recommendations** (for each runner-up)
6. **Risk flags** (if any)
7. **Optional: A/B test setup** — if scores are close, suggest what to measure and how long to run

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Picking a winner based on polish or production value alone — a rough UGC hook often beats a slick studio ad
- Confusing *emotional intensity* with *correct emotion* — crying at an ad doesn't mean clicking
- Ignoring the first 3 seconds — this is where 60-80% of drop-off happens on social
- Assuming longer = more persuasive — compression often increases conversion
- Forgetting sound-off scenarios for social (60%+ of mobile video watched muted)
- Over-indexing on clever copy and under-indexing on clarity
